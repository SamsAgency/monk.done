ABOUT US

Founded on Saturday August 22, 2015 in Brooklyn, NY, the Straight Black Pride KenyaKenyaMovement (SBPM) was designed to be a 21st Century platform and identity for Black people around the world. The simplistic name of the movement is designed to attract positive thinking Black people who share the principles the name and the movement promote. SBPM is an international movement created to celebrate and defend the traditional Afrikan family, which is composed of a Black man coupled with a Black woman who together conceive and gift the world with beautiful Black children. This position puts us in direct conflict with any efforts to partner the Black man with ANYONE other than a consenting Black woman and vice versa.

In the spirit of preserving the traditional Black family structure, the SBPM organized a tour to East Afrika in March of 2019 to prevent the legalization of homophilia (homosexuality and/or pedophilia) in Kenya. In January 2019, we became aware of millions of dollars being invested by the USA, Western nations, and the international lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender (LGBT) community to petition the Kenyan High Court to invalidate its current laws that criminalize “unnatural offences” and “indecent practices between males”. The Court consolidated these petitions into one case. These statutes effectively outlaw homosexual sex and bestiality. The petitioners claimed that the laws were vague and discriminatory. 

The international LGBT community in conjunction with a number of Western governments poured an estimated $3M dollars into a massive public relations campaign to pressure the High Court to rule in favor of legalizing homophilia. The SBPM raised $5K dollars to travel to East Afrika, and Kenya in particular, to educate the public about the international LGBT agenda to sexualize children and destabilize traditional Black family values. Our representative was Ayo Kimathi (aka: Irritated Genie of Soufeese). We organized in Uganda, Kenya, and Zimbabwe. Mr. Kimathi lectured at Makerere University in Uganda, the University of Nairobi in Kenya, and for various community and church groups in East Afrika.

Our most effective action appears to be informing the East Afrikan community about the meaning of the LGBT flag. Prior to our arrival, the Ugandan, Zimbabwean, and Kenyan public were largely unaware that the rainbow flag was an international symbol of homophilia. Mr. Kimathi lectured at the University of Nairobi on a Thursday night and informed the community what the LGBT flag represented. By Saturday morning, Kenyan papers published articles about the outrage Kenyans were expressing over the LGBT community painting rainbow flags on the University of Nairobi’s steps. These steps had been painted (2) months prior to Mr. Kimathi’s visit; however, upon investigation, it became clear that no one in the area had any idea what it represented

This revelation lead to a groundswell of grassroots mobilization where the people made it known that Kenya was not a place for the LGBT agenda to plant its flag and agenda. As a result of the SBPM tour and a coordinated effort of many Kenyan grassroot people and organizations, on Friday May 24, 2019, a three-judge panel unanimously rejected the petition to invalidate Kenya’s laws against homosexuality. The Justices affirmed the Afrikan way. Here are some excerpts from the Justices’ decision: 

• “We were invited not to be guided by public opinion or majoritarian views in determining this Petition. In our humble view, the desire of Kenyans, whether majoritarian or otherwise, are reflected in the Constitution. We are unable to agree with the Petitioners that the views of Kenyans should be ignored….” 

• “In our view, where the will of the people is expressed in the Constitution, it represents societal values….”

• “Article 45(2) [of the Kenyan Constitution] only recognizes marriage between adult persons of the opposite sex. In our view, decriminalizing same-sex sex on grounds that it is consensual and is done in private between adults, would contradict the express provisions of Article 45(2).”

• “We are not persuaded by arguments that the [disputed] sections are vague and unclear. It is dangerous to take that route since it is a mischievous way by the petitioners to legalize homosexuality against the express provisions of the Constitution….”
